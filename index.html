Area

The Misty Peaks (渓流) is a location introduced in Monster Hunter Portable 3rd. It is located in a temperate, mountainous region near Yukumo Village. This location features tall, rocky spires, lush forests of bamboo and deciduous trees, and several bodies of fresh water. It is home to many large monsters such as Arzuros, Rathian, Zinogre and Mizutsune.

An Arena that first appeared in Monster Hunter Portable 3rd. It consists of a very narrow space compared to other Arenas, including previous iterations of small arenas, something which may increase the difficulty of dodging monsters. It is usually seen in Hotspring quests. The fighting area consists of an opening on a canyon, you can see the desert which extends as far as the eyes can see, the base camp is a cave. Being a narrow space, only the smallest monsters appear here, such as the Bird Wyvern leaders, Great Jaggi, Great Baggi, Great Wroggi and their minions, along with Bulldrome, Arzuros, Lagombi and Volvidon.

The Lava Canyon is a new area introduced in Monster Hunter Portable 3rd. The only monster that inhabitats this area is Akantor. Cool Drinks are needed in this area to stop health depletion. The main area is an enclosed cave with lava flowing down various rock faces and in rivers.

The Polar Field is an arena-like area inhabited by powerful monsters like the Ukanlos, Rusted Kushala Daora, Grimclaw Tigrex, and Elderfrost Gammoth. This area is characterized by its steep ice cliffs and vast polar fields. An aurora can be seen in the night sky. The cave roof will release ice crystals when Ukanlos or other large monsters roars that can damage the hunter. Temperatures here are punishingly low, and so a hot drink must be carried at all times.

The Sacred Pinnacle is an area introduced in Monster Hunter Portable 3rd, located high in the mountains surrounding Yukumo Village. The sky in this area is blood red, with swirls of grey and purple. Roaring, gale-force winds can be heard at all times. Hunters can man the four ballistae in the area to shoot at or bind Amatsu in place.
